The Ranking System

Go has a very unique ranking system. Unlike Chess and the FIDE elo system, Go has a rather arbitrary way of comparing the skills of other players. All ranks start with a number and a sort of suffix after it. Amatuer player ranks have a "kyu" after the ranks, more advanced players have a "dan" after their rank, and professionals have a "p" after their rank. The numbers go from 30 kyu to 1 kyu before going up to 1dan to 10 dan (In kyu levels, lower is better. In dan levels, higher is better). These ranks are earned by demonstrating profiency in beating similarly ranked players, and being able to beat higher level players with limited handicaps. Professional ranks and promotions are earned by winning tournaments.

Example Progression

A new player would start at 30kyu. After a few months of practice they would be promoted to 9k. This is known as Single Digit Kyu (SDK), and many people mark this rank as the difference between beginner and intermediate players. After hundreds upon hundreds of hours of playing and studying theory, a player should be able to attain 1 dan. Depending on the country the player is from, this can be a big deal. After this level though, promotions become much more difficult to attain. The player will continue climbing up ranks instead of down. This means they will be promoted to 2 dan before 3 dan, unlike the kyu levels where promotions meant your number would go down (9kyu>30kyu, 3dan>2dan).
